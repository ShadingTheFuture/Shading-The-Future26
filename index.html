About Us

We are Hannah and Caoimhe Quinn, 16 year old twin sisters from Castleblayney College Co.Monaghan. Although we are twins, we each bring our own perspectives and interests, but we share a long-standing curiosity about how the world works. Growing up together has meant that learning, questioning and problem solving has always been apart of our lives. Our interest in science developed early, not only as a school subject but as a way of understanding systems, patterns and real world challenges. To us, science is driven by curiosity and the pursuit of evidence based answers. It involves asking difficult questions, analysing information critically and being willing to explore ideas even when the outcome is uncertain. Working together as twins has strengthened our ability to communicate clearly, divide tasks effectively, and rely on one another's strengths. These are skills that support both our academic work and personal development. Outside of science we both enjoy creative and reflective activities. Art provides a way to express ideas visually and think beyond fixed structure. Music is also a source of relaxation and a source of focus. Family plays a central role in our lives. We are two of four siblings, and we are extremely close as a family. Our parents have conistently supported our interests and encouraged us. Their support has been a key factor of this project. We have created Shading The Future in response to the growing need for UV protection solutions that do not compromise environmental health. With the rise in global temperatures and ozone thinning means that more UV radiation is present in our atmosphere which directly affects us. We wanted to use science to create someting that genuinely helps.
